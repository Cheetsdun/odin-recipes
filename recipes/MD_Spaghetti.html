Million Dollar Spaghetti

Description:

In Million Dollar Spaghetti, the ricotta layer lends an overall creamy texture, but it’s not too heavy or rich. A thick layer of meat sauce keeps everything loose. (If you’ve had a dry spaghetti bake, you’ll know what we mean.) Did we mention there's cheese? Four kinds of cheese are in this spaghetti casserole so that every bite is ooey and gooey.

Million Dollar Spaghetti ingredients

  • Spaghetti: You can use another pasta if you want, but the way the long, thin spaghetti noodles press together and bake really makes the texture of this casserole special.
  • Ground sirloin and ground sausage Feel free to use all of one kind of meat—or even reach for ground turkey or ground chicken as an alternative. The sausage and beef are a bit richer, but you'll never know the difference in the final dish.
  • Onion and garlicTo add flavor and aromatics.
  • Marinara nauce We prefer Rao's for its brightness and fresh flavor, but any will work so long as you like it.
  • Butter Covering the spaghetti in a bit of marinara and butter helps to avoid it turning dry while baking.
  • Cream cheese This forms the base of the creamy cheese layer. You could use more ricotta, or reach for flavored cream cheese, like garlic and herb.
  • Ricotta cheese This adds a lot of richness without too much flavor. Some people prefer cottage cheese ver ricotta, and that's a good swap if you happen to have cottage cheese already.
  • Parmesan cheese This finishes the three-cheese layer.
  • Mozzarella cheese Top the spaghetti casserole with mozzarella that will turn bubbly and golden in the oven.
  • Parsley Fresh parsley is a nice touch to finish off the dish.

Directions:

  1. Prepare oven and pan: Preheat oven to 350°F. Coat a 9- x 13-inch baking dish with cooking spray; set aside.
  2. Cook the spaghetti:
    3. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil over high. Add spaghetti, and cook according to package directions.
  3. Make the meat sauce: While pasta cooks, heat a large nonstick skillet over medium-high. Add ground sirloin and sausage. Cook, breaking up meat into smaller pieces with a wooden spoon and stirring occasionally, until meat is browned and no longer pink, about 6 minutes.
  4. Add onion and garlic: Add onion and garlic to meat mixture; cook, stirring occasionally, until onion is translucent, about 4 minutes.
  5. Add marinara: Stir 3 1/2 cups of the marinara into meat mixture. Remove from heat.
  6. Add butter and marinara to pasta: Add butter and remaining 1/2 cup marinara to pasta in pot.
  7. Make cheese mixture: Stir together cream cheese, ricotta, and Parmesan in a small bowl until smooth.
  8. Add meat sauce: Top pasta mixture with meat sauce.
  9. Bake the spaghetti: Bake in preheated oven until sauce is bubbling and cheese is golden, about 30 minutes. Let stand 10 minutes.
